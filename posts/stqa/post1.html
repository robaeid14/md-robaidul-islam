Objectives of Software Testing

Definition

Software testing is the process of evaluating and verifying that a software product or application does what it is supposed to do. The main aim is to identify defects and ensure the software meets the specified requirements.

Primary Objectives of Software Testing

  1. Verification and Validation
    • Verification: Are we building the product right?
    • Validation: Are we building the right product?
  2. Defect Detection

    To uncover defects, bugs, or faults in the software before delivery.

  3. Quality Assurance

    Ensure the software meets quality standards in terms of functionality, performance, reliability, usability, etc.

  4. Requirement Conformance

    Confirm that the software behaves according to the requirements specifications.

  5. Customer Satisfaction

    Deliver a reliable product that meets user expectations and provides a smooth experience.

  6. Risk Reduction

    Identify and eliminate potential risks or failures that may occur in the real-world environment.

  7. Improve Software Development Process

    Feedback from testing helps improve coding, design, and requirements gathering practices.

Additional Goals (Secondary Objectives)

  • Reduce cost of failure in production.
  • Support decision-making regarding software release.
  • Ensure software is maintainable, portable, and scalable.
  • Measure software attributes like performance, load handling, security, etc.

Exam Point Summary

The main objectives of software testing are:

  • To verify and validate software against requirements.
  • To detect defects and ensure product quality.
  • To increase customer satisfaction by delivering reliable software.
  • To reduce risks and avoid failures in production.
  • To support quality assurance and improve development practices.
